Your Queue

What is My Queue?

My Queue is your personal list of candidates waiting for a vote. It only shows candidates assigned to your reviewer group who you have not yet voted Yes or No on. Candidates you have waitlisted remain in the queue until you cast a final vote.

How do I navigate between candidates?

Use the Previous and Next buttons at the top and bottom of the candidate profile. These stay scoped to your current batch — switching batches in the filter resets navigation to the first candidate in that batch.

Why is a candidate missing from my queue?

A candidate disappears from your queue once you cast a Yes or No vote on them. If you believe a candidate is missing in error, check My Reviews — they may already have a recorded vote.

What does "Show waitlisted" do?

By default, candidates you have waitlisted are kept in your queue so you can revisit them. Turning off Show waitlisted hides them temporarily if you want to focus on candidates you have not touched yet.

{# ── Section: Voting ─────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Voting

What do Yes, No, and Waitlist mean?

Vote What it means Effect on queue
Yes You recommend this candidate for admission Removed from your queue
No You do not recommend this candidate Removed from your queue
Waitlist You are undecided — want to revisit Stays in your queue

Can I change my vote?

Yes. Find the candidate in My Reviews, open their profile, and cast a new vote. Your most recent vote is the one that counts.

What if I navigate away without voting?

A prompt will appear if you try to leave a candidate profile without voting. You can choose to leave anyway — no vote will be recorded — or stay and cast your vote first.

{# ── Section: Flagging ───────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Flagging

What does flagging a candidate do?

Flagging marks a candidate for committee discussion. Use it when something in the application warrants a conversation before a final decision is made — not as a substitute for voting. A comment is required when you flag.

Can other reviewers see my flag?

Admins can see all flags and comments. Other committee members can see that a candidate has been flagged but cannot read your specific comment — only you and admins can see what you wrote.

Can I remove a flag I placed?

Yes. Open the candidate profile and click the flag button again to remove your flag.

Reading the Candidate Info Card

GPA fields

Three GPA values are shown, each covering a different subset of coursework:

  • Overall GPA — all undergraduate coursework combined
  • Science GPA — natural science and math courses only
  • BCP GPA — Biology, Chemistry, and Physics courses only; the most heavily weighted by dental schools

GPAs are color-coded: green ≥ 3.5 · yellow ≥ 3.0 · red < 3.0.

DAT scores

DAT scores are on a scale of 200–600. The national average Academic Average is approximately 400. Competitive applicants typically score 430–460, and top-tier scores are 470 and above.

  • Academic Average — average of all DAT sections; the headline number
  • Total Science — combined score across Biology, General Chemistry, and Organic Chemistry
  • PAT — Perceptual Ability Test; measures spatial reasoning and hand-eye coordination relevant to dentistry
  • Quantitative Reasoning — math and problem-solving
  • Reading Comprehension — reading speed and retention
  • Biology, General Chem, Organic Chem — individual science section scores

Experience hours

Dental experience hours reflect hands-on clinical work (e.g. dental assisting). Shadowing hours reflect observation of a licensed dentist. Both are self-reported by the applicant on their AADSAS application.

AADSAS data (Total AI, NC, Z-Score)

Total AI (Academic Index) and Total NC (Non-Cognitive score) are composite metrics calculated by AADSAS. Z-Score is a standardized score relative to the applicant pool for that cycle. Higher is better for all three.

Scores

What is the reviewer score?

The reviewer score is an optional numeric rating you can assign to a candidate alongside your vote. It captures your overall impression and is used in the stats strip on My Reviews and in admin analytics. Check with the admissions director for the scoring rubric your group uses.

Is a score required?

No. You can vote without entering a score. If you do enter one, it is factored into your average score shown on My Reviews.

{# ── Section: My Reviews ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

My Reviews

What does the stats strip show?

The strip at the top of My Reviews summarises your voting activity across the current filter:

  • Yes / No / Waitlist — count of each vote type you have cast
  • Flagged — candidates you have flagged for discussion
  • Avg Score — your average reviewer score across voted candidates (blank if no scores entered)
  • Vote bar — a visual breakdown of Yes / No / Waitlist proportions

Can I export My Reviews?

Yes. Use the Export CSV button at the top of My Reviews to download a spreadsheet of your votes, scores, and candidate details matching the current filter.

Deadlines

What do the deadline colors mean?

The deadline urgency strip on your dashboard uses color to signal how much time is left:

  • Red — Critical: deadline is 3 days away or less. Review immediately.
  • Yellow — Soon: deadline is within 7 days. Plan to finish this week.
  • Green — On track: more than 7 days remaining.

What happens if I miss a deadline?

Batches remain open after their deadline — your votes still count. However, final decisions may proceed without your input if you have not voted by the time the committee convenes. Contact the admissions director if you need an extension.

{# ── Section: Finishing Up ───────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Finishing Up

What happens when I finish all my candidates?

When you vote on the last candidate in a batch, a completion card appears showing your vote breakdown for that batch — how many Yes, No, and Waitlist votes you cast. From there you can return to your dashboard or continue to any remaining batches.

What if new candidates are added after I finish?

Your dashboard will show a prompt when new batches are uploaded that include candidates assigned to your group. Your queue will reflect the new candidates automatically.

Do I need to do anything after voting?

No action is needed beyond casting your votes. The admissions team reviews all committee votes when making final decisions. You will not be notified of the outcome unless someone from the admissions team reaches out directly.

{# ── Section: Contact ────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

Something looks wrong

A candidate's information looks incorrect

Candidate data is imported from AADSAS applications. If a GPA, DAT score, or personal detail looks wrong, do not adjust your vote based on it — contact the admissions director and note the candidate's name and what looks incorrect. They can verify against the source document.

I can't access a candidate I should be able to see

Access is controlled by your reviewer group assignment. If a candidate you expect to see is missing from your queue entirely and is not in My Reviews, contact the admissions director — you may need to be added to the correct group for that batch.

I found a bug or something broken

Contact the admissions director with a description of what you were doing and what happened. If you can include a screenshot, that helps.
